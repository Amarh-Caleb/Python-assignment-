      The results show that as we increase the width of the well (a), the energy eigenvalues decrease and the wavefunctions become more spread out. This makes sense because a wider well corresponds to a lower potential energy and a higher probability of finding the particle in a larger region of space. On the other hand, as we increase the height of the potential barrier (V_0), the energy eigenvalues increase and the wavefunctions become more confined to the well region. This is because a higher potential barrier corresponds to a higher potential energy and a lower probability of finding the particle outside the well region.
